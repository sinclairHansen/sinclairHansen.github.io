Lorenz Attractor Simulator

The Lorenz attractor is a system of chaotic solutions to a set of differential equations originally developed by Edward Lorenz while modeling atmospheric convection. It is one of the earliest and most famous examples of deterministic chaos, where small changes in starting conditions lead to vastly different outcomes. This visualization shows the iconic butterfly-shaped structure formed by plotting the systemâ€™s evolving state over time. Even though the Lorenz system is governed by simple rules, its motion is highly unpredictable, beautifully illustrating the heart of chaos theory.
